On Sundays and [public holidays](/guides/public-holidays), most shops in Berlin must close, but there are exceptions. This guide shows you where to buy groceries and other things when all the stores are closed.

{% tableOfContents %}

## What stays open

These businesses can open on Sundays:[^3]

- Shops in train stations, airports and bus terminals
- Restaurants and food delivery
- Cafés
- Bakeries
- Many *[Spätis](/glossary/Sp%C3%A4ti)*[^4]
- Petrol stations
- Pharmacies (*Apotheken*)
- Tourist attractions and souvenir shops
- Flea markets and art markets
- Museums

Grocery delivery services are closed on Sundays.[^1]

### Restaurants, Spätis and flea markets

Most restaurants open on Sundays. Food delivery services also work.

Most *[Spätis](/glossary/Sp%C3%A4ti)* open on Sundays.

[Many flea markets](https://www.berlin.de/special/shopping/flohmaerkte/) (*Flohmarkt*) only open on Sundays.

### Museums

Some museums and tourist attractions stay open on Sundays.

## What is open on public holidays?

If a business can open on Sundays, it can open on public holidays. Some businesses still choose to close on public holidays.

**[List of public holidays in Berlin ➞](/guides/public-holidays)**

## Sunday shopping days

There are up to 8 Sunday shopping days (*Verkaufsoffene Sonntage*) per year. On those Sundays, shops can open from 13:00 to 20:00. Those days are announced in advance on Berlin.de.

**[List of Sunday shopping days](https://www.berlin.de/special/shopping/sonntag/1784369-1742809-verkaufsoffene-sonntage-in-berlin.html)** - Berlin.de

These shops usually open on Sunday shopping days:

- Most shopping malls
- Some furniture stores
- Some electronics stores
- Some supermarkets
